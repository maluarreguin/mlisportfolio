Accesibility Statement

This Digital Portfolio was designed intentionally to be as accessible as possible for all users. As an information professional, the need for equity of information lies at the forefront of decision making. Throughout my time in the program, I have learned the significance of usable design practices to allow ease of access for all. This includes those of varying abled bodies as well as those who could lack adequate tools to view this digital portfolio in the primary format it was designed. Thus, I chose to adhere to the overtly recognized Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, which provide a comprehensive set of standards for digital accessibility. To adhere to these accommodations, this website has been deliberately designed to abide by the following aspects—arrangement, downloadable file formats, and concise design features. Regarding arrangement, my website has organized each section of the portfolio accordingly in the top bar section featured on each page of this website. This arrangement follows a natural path and incorporates submenus to ensure ease for users. Further, each section possesses a downloadable PDF version of that page’s content, placed at the top of each page to ensure ease of findability. Each PDF opens as a separate tab as well, allowing users to not be displaced from their original path. Finally, this website was designed to be readable and accessible above all else. This means black text in a readable font against a white a background can be found on each page, with the picture of UCLA used as the featured image of the site showcasing the menu bar with white text against the light blue background of the sky, but nonetheless, this remains readable to the average user.
