Coursework

The following data visualizations emphasize numerous points as taken from the dataset including years of cases, genders of those accused, and the total count of cases involving fairies within the database at large.

The chart above is derived from the year count in the database of the accused whose cases incolve faires. Here,  we see the most prominent year being 1697, with 1661 and 1597 trailing behind in the sceond and third slots respectively. The year 1697 amounts to a total of 20 incidents, with 1661 standing at 12 incidents and 1597 at 11 incidents. As previously stated, I theorized that the awareness of a date, or rather in this case, ending of their respective centuries, brings forth stranger and more susceptible behaivors than usual from the societies that are living through it. The change is coming, and thus this foreseeable change enacts a literal change on those confined within it. 

Out of the 113 records found from the Survey of Scottish Witchcraft Database, 96 of the accused were women leaving 17 of the accused to be men. As we can see from the pie chart, this breaks down to 85% versus 15%. Regarding the entire data set at large, of the apparently 4,000 records, we only have gender recorded for about 3,200 cases, and from this, the breakdown aligns congruently with what has already been documented with 2702 cases involving women and 468 involving men. This in turn amounts to 85% of the accused in the survey to be women versus approximately 15% being men. 

Within the database there are roughly 4,000 records of accused persons. Within that, only 113 records involved fairies, amounting to approximately 3% of the total. As we can see, fairies were a relatively miniscule factor in the cases of the accused, but its occurence can allow for a small-scale investigation that could potentially expose insights to the greater dataset at large.
